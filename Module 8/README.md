# Module 08 - Cybersecurity and AI in Telecommunications

**Course:** ITAI 4370 - AI in 5G and 6G Communications and ORAN Networks  
**Student:** Monica Joya  |  **Term:** Summer 2026  
**Module dates:** July 21 - July 27, 2026

## Overview

Module 08 covered how 5G and 6G widen the attack surface and how AI helps defend against it. It ran from classic network threats through the newer risks of cloud-based, sliced 5G cores, then into using machine learning to detect and respond to attacks, and finished on keeping that AI trustworthy and explainable. This was a study module built around the lecture, with the practical side shown through an intrusion detection walkthrough.

## Focus of this module

The threats that come with 5G and 6G, how AI detects and responds to them, and how to keep the defending AI trustworthy. Threats include eavesdropping, denial of service, rogue base stations, and downgrade attacks that force a fallback to weaker 4G.

## What I learned

What I learned is that 5G and 6G widen the attack surface: cloud-based cores, slicing, edge computing, and large device counts each add new entry points, on top of older weaknesses. AI fits network defense because it can detect anomalies and attacks in traffic and automate the response, using supervised models for known attacks and unsupervised models for unknown ones. The AI itself also has to be defended, since adversarial machine learning can trick or poison a model, which is why explainable AI tools like LIME and SHAP matter, so an analyst can trust and check the alerts.
