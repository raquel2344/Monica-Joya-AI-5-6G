# Module 09 - Sustainable AI and Green Communications

**Course:** ITAI 4370 - AI in 5G and 6G Communications and ORAN Networks  
**Student:** Monica Joya  |  **Term:** Summer 2026

## Overview

Module 09 covered the energy cost of networks and how AI can reduce it. Base stations use most of the power, so the module focused on measuring energy use, designing greener networks, and using AI to manage power without hurting service quality. This was a study module on network energy, its metrics, and the AI methods that reduce it.

## Focus of this module

Where network energy goes, how it is measured, and how AI brings it down. Base stations are the largest draw, around sixty to eighty percent of the power, and cooling adds a large share on top. Efficiency is tracked with metrics like PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness).

## Readings that informed this module

- Sustainability in 6G networks (journal article)
- Energy-efficient AI for wireless networks (arXiv 2101.01588)

## What I learned

I learned that AI reduces energy in concrete ways: forecasting demand so cells can sleep when traffic is light, adjusting power dynamically, and optimizing cooling, with reinforcement learning treating it as a trade-off between saving energy and keeping service quality.
